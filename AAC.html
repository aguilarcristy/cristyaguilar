Girlhood: Aprende A Cocinar

"Aprende A Cocinar"
Made in Adobe Illustrator

Aprende A Cocinar is part of the "Girlhood" series. Inspired by cultural expectations and "norms" for Mexican women, this poster was catalyzed by a relative's comment when I was around 16 to 17 years old. In order to encourage me to learn how to cook, I was told "Sabias que algunos hombres les avientan los trastes a sus esposas si no les gusta su comida?"

Did you know some men throw their plates at their wives if they don't like the food they cooked for them?

Shocked by the fact that I was encouraged to learn how to cook to avoid domestic violence rather than be taught to never tolerate any form of abuse from a partner, this quote was engrained in my brain and has been something I still remember four to five years later.

When I think of my childhood - girlhood, if you will - I think of all the things I was taught would be expected from me. To cook, to be a docile wife and most likely mother.

As I have grown older, I have realized the detriment of these expectations and I have come to realize I decide what I want to be as an adult.

Girlhood is being told you'll have plates thrown in your face if your husband doesn't like the food you cook.
